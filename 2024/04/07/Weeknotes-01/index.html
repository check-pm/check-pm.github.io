Weeknote 01

Chris Heck
2024-04-07
Weeknotes

Week of April 1-7, 2024
The big event this week was PI Planning. This was the 12th PI Planning exercise the company has completed since adopting SAFe and the 9th since I’ve been onboard. Although they have certainly improved and gone much more smoothly over time, this one was especially troubling for the broader product org. The majority of the frustration and stress seemed to come from some last minute churn introduced by leadership. I do find it quite ironic that leadership seems to be relatively passive until right before our planning sessions. It’s like they try to wait to communicate and introduce change until the most inopportune time. Many of the teams seemed especially put out this go round.

My team’s planning went relatively well and without a hitch. Of course, this is primarily due to the reality that we received word a few weeks ago that following this upcoming PI, we will be putting our product/team in mainteance mode while we redploy capacity and focus to other priorities. Consequently, we only have this PI to focus on completing two key product updates that will position us to reach product market fit. Which again, is ironic that right as we get our product to the point where it meets the critical needs of our target users, we will be backing off on investing in it. But, it could be worse I guess. They could use this decision as a way to cut staff. That is the good news here. It’s been communicate that we will not reduce headcount. We are simply redeploying to other priorities.

In addition to planning our work for the quarter ahead, I also started reading the next book that our book club select. The book is Hidden Potential by Adam Grant. So far, I’m really enjoying it. I’m only a couple chapters in so far but am already struck by the tension that exists between our natural human inclination to seek comfort and the reality that the best way to accelerate growth is to embrace, seek, and amplify discomfort.

And, not to bury the lede but, I’m obviously testing out a new habit; publishing weeknotes. Let’s just say I stumbled upon a Medium article about them and then, after linking to multiple examples (all people from the UK quite interestingly), decided to try it myself. If you are looking for more information, I suggest this new resource put together by Giles Turnbull. I’m not sure if this will take for me and I have no idea how my approach to them might evolve, but for this first attempt I’m simply going with a freeform approach while laying in bed trying to recover from the flu.

I’m also using my maiden weeknote voyage as an opportunity to test a blog framework and GitHub Pages. What I love about this combination is that they both are offered at a great price ($Free). So, again, a low-risk, low barrier to entry proposition.

Now, here’s hoping I can recover from this illness that has had me in bed all weekend so I can tackle the week ahead. Cheers!
